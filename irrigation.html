All about Irrigation

Surface Irrigation

Surface irrigation is one of the oldest and most widely used methods in India. It involves flooding or channeling water over the soil surface, allowing it to infiltrate and reach the root zone of crops. Common surface irrigation techniques include basin irrigation, furrow irrigation, and border irrigation. Surface irrigation is suitable for a wide range of crops, especially in areas with flat terrain and abundant water supply.

Drip Irrigation

Drip irrigation is a water-efficient method that delivers water directly to the root zone of plants through a network of tubes, pipes, and emitters. This system minimizes water wastage by delivering precise amounts of water to individual plants or crop rows, reducing evaporation and runoff. Drip irrigation is particularly well-suited for areas with water scarcity, saline soils, or uneven terrain. Although initial installation costs may be higher, drip irrigation can lead to significant water savings and improved crop yields over the long term.

Sprinkler Irrigation

Sprinkler irrigation involves spraying water over the soil surface in the form of droplets, simulating natural rainfall. Sprinkler systems consist of pipes, pumps, and sprinkler heads that distribute water evenly across the field. This method is suitable for a wide range of crops, including field crops, fruits, vegetables, and nurseries. Sprinkler irrigation is effective in areas with high wind speeds, sandy soils, or where water needs to be distributed over a large area.

Subsurface Irrigation

Subsurface irrigation involves delivering water directly to the root zone of plants through buried pipes, tubes, or porous materials installed below the soil surface. This method minimizes water loss due to evaporation and runoff, as well as reduces weed growth and soil erosion. Subsurface irrigation is suitable for crops with shallow root systems or in areas with waterlogging issues. While installation costs may be higher, subsurface irrigation can improve water-use efficiency and crop yields, especially in regions with limited water resources.

Lift Irrigation

Lift irrigation is a method of lifting water from a water source, such as a river, canal, or reservoir, to higher elevations using pumps or mechanical devices. This system is commonly used in areas where natural water sources are located at lower elevations or where water needs to be transported over long distances. Lift irrigation helps farmers access water for irrigation in areas with limited groundwater availability or during dry seasons. While lift irrigation can be effective, it requires energy inputs for pumping water, which can increase operational costs for farmers.
