All about Farming Seasons

Rabi Season

Rabi season generally begins in October and lasts until March or April. It coincides with the winter months in India, characterized by cooler temperatures and lower humidity levels. Crops sown during the Rabi season typically require less water and are harvested in the spring. Major Rabi crops include wheat, barley, oats, chickpeas (chana), mustard, peas, and various fruits and vegetables like carrots, potatoes, onions, and tomatoes. These crops are usually sown in October-November after the monsoon rains have ceased and are harvested in April-May.

Kharif Season

Kharif season spans from June to September and coincides with the southwest monsoon season in India. This season is characterized by high temperatures, humidity, and heavy rainfall in many parts of the country. Kharif crops are typically sown with the onset of monsoon rains and harvested before the arrival of winter. Major Kharif crops include rice, maize, sorghum (jowar), pearl millet (bajra), cotton, sugarcane, groundnuts, soybeans, and pulses like pigeon peas (tur) and mung beans (moong). These crops are sown in June-July and harvested between September and November.

Zaid Season

Zaid season is a short-duration cropping season that falls between the Rabi and Kharif seasons. It typically occurs during the summer months, from March to June. Zaid crops are often grown in areas with irrigation facilities and include crops like watermelon, muskmelon, cucumber, bitter gourd, pumpkin, and leafy vegetables. Zaid crops have a shorter growing period and are usually harvested within a few months of sowing.
